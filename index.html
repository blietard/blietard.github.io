Bastien Liétard

bastien [dot] lietard [dot] etu [at] univ-lille [dot] fr

French M2 student (he/they) in Data Science at the University of Lille and Centrale Lille engineering school (France). Aspiring researcher, focused on Computational Linguistics and Natural Language Processing.

As the topic of my master thesis for this second year, I am working on computational approaches to lexical semantic change detection, with Pascal Denis and Mikaela Keller (INRIA MAGNET team). The idea is to model the evolution of a word's sense(s) over time and detect whenever there is a significant change in usage/meaning. Further work would be to characterize this change more precisely, and also to extend the scope to changes that are beyond Semantics (e.g. Morphosyntax).

In 2021 I did a research intership in the CoAStAL group at the University of Copenhagen, under the supervision of Anders Søgaard. My reseach was about probing Language Models for geographic knowledge (see the paper).

During my B.Sc and my first year of Master, I worked on Language Modeling and Semantics of adjectives, with a focus on so-called privative adjectives. This could be ranged under a specific type of adjective-noun compositionality.

Research interests: Semantics, Pragmatics, Diachronic Linguistics, Statistics and Machine Learning.

Apart from work, I am writing tabletop RPGs and my own novel, and am interested in fantasy and sci-fi (especially cyberpunk) cultures, poetry, and 13th century european history.
